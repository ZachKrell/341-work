Alexander: The Mountain Man

Alexander

The Mountain Man

By Zachary Krell

There is this man who lives out in the mountains named Alexander...Do not, I repeat, do not call him Alex. A big, burly man and looks like he could tear you apart if he wanted too.

Alexander would never do that thought, he loved all creatures. Alexander was a good man, and he led a simple life. he hunted for his own food, built his own shelter, and lived completely off the grid. Alexander was happy.

It was about a year ago that I met Alexander, I was hiking in the wood and found his cabin. I thought that I was pretty far away form any civilization...Who's cabin was this? Naturally invasive as I am I knocked on the door, and out popped this giant of a man yelling "Yes! Who are you!?" As he was holding a knife covered in blood.

Of course I was taken back and thought I was about to die. I suppose that a natural reaction would have been to run, but something about him made me stay. I assume it was because he was wearing an apron that had garfield saying "I Hate Mondays." on it. Alexander immediately realize how terrifying he must have looked, and put the knife down and stepped outside.

In a voice that was still way to loud Alexander expressed regret and asked what i was doing there. I explained to him that I just stummbled upon his cabin and was curious about it. Alexander, the ever gracious host, offered to let me stick around and eat some of the meat he was preparing. I agreed curious to learn more about this strange man.

Sitting around a fire that he stared impossibly fast, he told me why he was in the mountains. He explained, still obnoxiously loud, "Yeah, one day I was on hod with the cable company and I just lost it...I couldn't deal with society anymore! So I left and came out here! I love it! I just couldn't deal with the rat race anymore!" I asked, "what do you do for fun?" "I race rats!" he said as he stood up and gestured for me to follow him.

He showed me to the back of his cabin where he had a whole race track set up for these rats that had numbers painted on their sides. He rang a bell and sure enough the rats began racing. As other woodland creatures came to watch as if it was their Super Bowl. "Best decision I ever made!" Alexander smiled.
